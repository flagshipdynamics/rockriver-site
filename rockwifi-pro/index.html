Wi-Fi tools that belong on every tech's laptop.

Whether you're surveying a warehouse, tracking down a rogue AP, or documenting coverage for a client, RockWiFi Pro puts professional-grade Wi-Fi analysis in a single desktop app. No cloud accounts. No annual licenses. No sending your client's floor plans to someone else's servers.

What RockWiFi Pro Does

📡

Dashboard

Live Wi-Fi status at a glance — SSID, signal strength (% and dBm), band, channel, and a scrolling signal chart. Quick-launch buttons for every tool.

🗺

Wi-Fi Survey & Heatmaps

Click-to-sample heatmaps on imported floor plans or OpenStreetMap tiles. Kriging interpolation, multi-sample clustering, and a guided tour mode for consistent coverage walks.

🔭

AP Locator

Hot/cold signal gauge to physically walk to any access point. Pin positions on a floor plan for trilateration. Channel congestion chart shows co-channel interference at a glance.

🔌

Connection Check

Full connectivity diagnostic — adapter, gateway, internet, DNS, latency, jitter, and packet loss. One click to find out exactly where the connection breaks.

🎯

Target Test

Probe a specific host or port with ping, TCP connect, HTTP, traceroute, and TLS certificate inspection. Know whether the problem is the network or the service.

📚

DNS Compare

Query multiple DNS resolvers side-by-side — system, Google, Cloudflare, Quad9, or custom — and compare results. Spot DNS-level filtering, hijacking, or stale records instantly.

📈

Latency Monitor

Continuous ping chart over time with jitter, loss, and percentile stats. Leave it running during a meeting and come back with proof of intermittent drops.

⏺

Flight Recorder

Background logging of connectivity metrics for post-incident analysis. Capture what happened while you were away — latency spikes, packet loss bursts, DNS failures.

📄

Reports & History

Browse past results, export HTML or text reports for client deliverables. Professional output without extra formatting work.

How People Use RockWiFi Pro

Client Site Surveys

Import a floor plan, walk the site with guided tour mode, and generate a heatmap showing signal coverage. Export an HTML report the client can read without installing anything.

Troubleshooting Calls

Run Connection Check to isolate the problem layer in seconds. Follow up with Target Test, DNS Compare, or Latency Monitor depending on what the diagnostic surfaces. Arrive at the answer before the user finishes describing the problem.

Post-Incident Documentation

Leave Flight Recorder running overnight or over a weekend. Review the timeline of latency spikes, packet loss, and DNS failures. Export the data and attach it to the ticket.

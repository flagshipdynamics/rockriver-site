Reading Findings and Recommendations

RockWiFi Pro Support

Home › RockWiFi Pro › Support › Reading Findings and Recommendations

RockWiFi Pro automatically analyzes your Wi-Fi environment and generates findings — specific, evidence-backed insights about issues, risks, and opportunities. Each finding includes a severity level and a recommended action so you know exactly what to address and in what order.

What Are Findings?

Findings are automated insights generated by analyzing the data collected from your Wi-Fi scans, surveys, and diagnostic tests. Each finding identifies a specific issue with supporting evidence, a severity rating, and a clear recommended action.

Severity Levels

Critical

Issues with immediate impact on users. Examples:

  • No internet connectivity detected
  • All APs on overlapping channels causing severe interference
  • Extremely weak coverage in occupied areas

Warning

Significant issues that should be addressed soon. Examples:

  • Legacy 802.11g APs dragging down network performance
  • High channel utilization on primary channels
  • Weak coverage in some areas of the surveyed space

Info

Opportunities and observations that are not urgent. Examples:

  • Wi-Fi 7 upgrade opportunity available
  • 6 GHz band not currently in use
  • MLO (Multi-Link Operation) detected on some APs

Finding Categories

  • Coverage — Dead zones, weak signal areas, coverage gaps between APs.
  • Congestion — Channel overlap, high utilization, too many APs on the same channel.
  • Security — WPA2 without WPA3, open networks, mixed security modes.
  • Standards — Legacy APs (802.11n/g), mixed standard deployments, upgrade opportunities.
  • Wi-Fi 7 — Partial Wi-Fi 7 deployment, 6 GHz not utilized, MLO opportunities, upgrade paths.
  • Configuration — Narrow channel widths, suboptimal band steering, beacon interval issues.

Actionable Recommendations

Every finding includes specific next steps. RockWiFi Pro does not just tell you to “improve coverage” — it tells you to “add an AP near the northeast corner where signal drops below −75 dBm.” Recommendations are grounded in the data collected during your scan or survey so they are specific to your environment.

Tips

  • Address critical findings first — these have immediate impact on user experience.
  • Warning-level findings are your next priority and typically represent the best opportunities for measurable improvement.
  • Info-level findings about Wi-Fi 7 are forward-looking. They help plan future upgrades rather than fix current problems.
  • Export findings in a report to share with clients or your team for remediation planning.

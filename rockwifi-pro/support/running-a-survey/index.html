Running Your First Wi-Fi Survey

What is a Wi-Fi survey?

A Wi-Fi site survey maps wireless signal coverage across a physical space. Walk through the space, drop signal samples at your location on a floor plan, and RockWiFi Pro builds a heatmap showing coverage quality everywhere.

Step 1: Open Wi-Fi Survey

Click Wi-Fi Survey in the sidebar. You'll see the survey canvas — a blank workspace ready for your floor plan.

Step 2: Import a floor plan

Click Import Floor Plan and select an image (PNG, JPG, BMP). This becomes your survey background. You can also use an OpenStreetMap capture for outdoor surveys.

Step 3: Calibrate

Click Calibrate, then click two points on the map whose real-world distance you know (e.g., a hallway length). Enter the distance in meters. This lets the app calculate accurate coverage areas and cluster nearby samples.

See Calibrating Your Floor Plan for detailed instructions.

Step 4: Drop samples

Walk to a location, click that spot on the floor plan. RockWiFi Pro captures your current Wi-Fi signal strength and all visible APs at that point. The sample appears as a colored dot.

Step 5: Build coverage

Take 15–30 samples across the space. Cover corners, hallways, and problem areas. More samples = more accurate heatmap. If you click near an existing sample (within ~1.5m), the readings are merged for better accuracy.

Step 6: View the heatmap

The heatmap renders automatically using Kriging interpolation. Colors range from red (weak signal) through yellow to green (strong signal). Switch between layers:

  • Signal Strength
  • Sample Density
  • SNR
  • Channel Overlap
  • AP Count

See Understanding Heatmap Layers for a full explanation of each layer.

Step 7: Save and export

Save the survey for later review. Export an HTML report for clients or a CSV of raw sample data.

Tips

  • Survey during normal business hours to capture real interference.
  • Walk at a normal pace.
  • Include areas behind walls and in stairwells.
  • Run UltraScan first to understand the environment.

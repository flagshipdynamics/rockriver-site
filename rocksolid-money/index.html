In Development

Local-first personal finance for Windows.

RockSolid Money is a desktop application for managing personal finances. It imports transactions from banks, credit cards, and brokerages, and tracks accounts, budgets, cash flow, investments, and net worth. Your records are stored in a file on your own machine.

A modern replacement for Microsoft Money and Quicken.

RockSolid Money follows the model those applications established: financial records live in a file on your own computer rather than in a hosted service. It reads the export formats banks and brokerages actually produce, checks each file against your account balances before anything is recorded, and proposes changes for your review rather than writing them straight to the ledger.

What RockSolid Money Does

Import From Banks and Brokerages

Reads CSV, OFX, QFX, and QIF exports from banks, credit cards, and brokerages. The app determines the file's column layout, checks its sign convention against the running balance, and proposes transactions for review. Nothing is written to the ledger until you approve it.

Accounts

Checking, savings, credit cards, loans, cash, and investment accounts in one place, with balances kept in a single local file rather than spread across separate bank logins.

Transactions and Transfers

A transaction register with categories, payees, memos, tags, and splits. Transfers between your own accounts are matched automatically, and re-importing a statement does not create duplicates.

Budgets

Set budgets by category and track actual spending against them as transactions are imported. Compare planned to actual for any period, with each figure traceable to the transactions behind it.

Cash Flow

Income and expenses summarized by month. Recurring items are identified and projected forward, so an upcoming shortfall is visible before it happens.

Bills and Reminders

Track recurring bills and subscriptions with due-date reminders, and review what is scheduled in the period ahead.

Investments and Holdings

Import brokerage activity and track holdings lot by lot with cost basis. Stocks, ETFs, bonds, and crypto are recorded per lot, with quantities and basis carried through to valuation and tax reporting.

Net Worth

Assets and liabilities totaled and tracked over time, with history by month so the effect of paying down debt or adding savings is visible as a trend.

Reports

Spending by category and payee, income and expenses, monthly cash flow, account balances, and net worth history. Every total drills through to the exact transactions it was computed from.

Additional Capabilities

Tax Center

Capital gains computed per lot, with long-term and short-term separated as Form 8949 requires. Retirement accounts are excluded from taxable gains. Where cost basis is unknown it is reported as unknown rather than as zero.

Investment Performance

Realized and unrealized gains calculated from the same per-lot records the tax reports use, so performance and tax figures are derived from one set of numbers.

Asset Allocation

Portfolio composition by asset class and account, consolidated across every investment account in the file.

Loans and Debt

Mortgages, auto loans, and other liabilities tracked alongside assets so net worth reflects both sides of the balance sheet.

Insights

Observations about spending patterns, recurring charges, and unusual activity. These are suggestions for review; the application does not reclassify or record anything on your behalf.

Document Vault

Attach statements, receipts, and tax documents to the accounts and transactions they relate to, stored locally alongside the ledger.

How Importing Works

Files are evidence, not transactions

An imported row is a claim about what happened, not a ledger entry. Each row is interpreted, matched against existing records, and validated before it is proposed for review. Input that cannot be interpreted unambiguously is reported rather than guessed at.

Format detection

For an unrecognized CSV, the app proposes a column mapping, determines the sign convention by testing it against the running balance, and infers the date format from the data itself. Confirm it once and later files from that institution use the saved profile.

Re-import safety

Importing the same statement twice does not create duplicate transactions. Fields you have edited — category, memo, payee, reconciliation status — are not overwritten by a later import. Commits are applied atomically and can be rolled back.

Common Workflows

Switching banks

Export history from the previous bank and import the file. The app proposes a column mapping, verifies the sign convention against the balances in the file, and brings the history across. Later exports from the new bank import against the saved profile.

Reconciling a statement

Import the period's activity, match it against the existing register, and clear each transaction against the statement balance. Duplicates are detected automatically, and anything ambiguous is flagged for review rather than resolved silently.

Preparing for tax season

Produce realized capital gains separated into long-term and short-term, one line per lot, with retirement accounts excluded from the taxable total. Keep the related 1099s in the document vault with the accounts they belong to.

What You're Getting

Local Data

Accounts, transactions, and documents are stored in a file on your machine. No cloud sync and no hosted account.

No Subscription

One-time purchase. The license is yours, with no annual renewal and no recurring fees.

No Telemetry

The application does not send telemetry, analytics, or usage data, and does not transmit your transactions.

Exact Arithmetic

Money is handled with decimal arithmetic rather than floating point. Investment lots, contract multipliers, and cost basis are computed per lot so totals reconcile.

You Approve Changes

The application suggests, matches, and flags. Every proposed ledger change is yours to approve, edit, or reject.

Direct Support

Support is handled by the developers who build the product, by email.

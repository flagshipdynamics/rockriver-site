Net Worth Tracking

RockSolid Money Support

Home › RockSolid Money › Support › Net Worth Tracking

Analyze > Net Worth states the one relationship the page exists for: total assets minus total liabilities, with the breakdown behind each side and the trend over time.

The breakdown

Assets (Cash & Bank, Securities, Crypto, Property) and Liabilities (Credit Cards, Loans) each show their amount and share of the total. A card in credit shows as −$386.03 credit balance — visibly reducing what you owe. Unpriced or unconfirmed holdings are named in a review strip, never silently omitted.

Property and other manual values

A house's market value isn't an account with transactions. + Add Asset / + Add Liability record household-entered values (the house, a private loan) that fold into the totals.

Snapshots build the trend

The trend chart draws from recorded snapshots — use Record today's value (in the trend panel's menu) at whatever cadence you like; monthly works well. With no price feed, recorded history is the only honest trend: nothing is interpolated or invented. The chart shows the change over the selected range, marks the latest point, and answers hover with each snapshot's date and value.

Spendable cash

Beneath the summary, Spendable cash answers a different question than net worth: what could you actually spend today? It covers cash accounts only — investments and property never count, and cryptocurrency is excluded unless you deliberately opt it in (Configure…).

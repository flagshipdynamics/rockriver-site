Updates and Support Policy

What's included with your RockTerm purchase.

Home › RockTerm › Support › Updates and Support Policy

What's included with your purchase

Your RockTerm license includes all updates — bug fixes, security patches, and new features — for the major version you purchased (currently version 1.x). There are no renewal fees, no annual maintenance charges, and no feature tiers within a major version. Every 1.x update is delivered automatically through the Microsoft Store at no additional cost.

Major version upgrades

If a future major version is released (e.g., RockTerm 2.0), it may be offered as a separate paid upgrade. Major version upgrades are entirely optional — your existing version will continue to work and receive security patches regardless of whether you upgrade. We will announce any major version upgrade well in advance, along with pricing and what's new.

To be clear: we have no plans to release a major version upgrade at this time. Version 1.x is actively developed and receives new features regularly.

Security patches

We commit to releasing security patches for the current major version for as long as it is the actively sold version. Security vulnerabilities in RockTerm's own code or in bundled dependencies will be addressed promptly. Security patches are delivered through the same Microsoft Store auto-update mechanism as all other updates.

Email support

Email support is available to all licensed users at info@rockriverresearch.com. You're writing directly to the people who build the product — there are no support tiers, no ticket queues, and no chatbots.

We aim to respond within one business day for most inquiries. As a small team, we do not guarantee a specific response time or offer a formal SLA. If you're experiencing a blocking issue, say so in your email and we'll prioritize accordingly.

What happens if development stops

RockTerm is a fully local application. It does not depend on any server, cloud service, or online activation system to function. Your connections, settings, credentials, and scripts are stored entirely on your machine. The only network calls the app makes are the ones you initiate — SSH, Telnet, Serial, SFTP, and optionally the AI assistant (which uses your own API key).

This means that even in the unlikely event that Rock River Research ceases operations, RockTerm continues to work indefinitely. There is no license server to go offline, no subscription to lapse, and no cloud dependency to break. Your purchased copy is yours.

This is a deliberate architectural decision, not an accident. We believe that tools for managing production infrastructure should not have a single point of failure outside your control.
